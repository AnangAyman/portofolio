Flagship Project

SignLingo AI

Democratizing sign language education with a lightweight, accessible AI tutor that runs on any device.

Role

Team Lead & AI Engineer

Timeline

1 year (ongoing)

Team Size

10 Developers

Core Tech

CNN-LSTM, Docker, Flask

The Challenge

Learning sign language in Indonesia is prohibitively expensive due to the high cost of private tutors. The only free alternatives are passive methods like static videos, where learners receive no feedback on whether they are signing correctly.

Humans are social creatures, and I believe communication is a fundamental right—being disabled should not mean being disconnected from society. However, while modern Computer Vision is accurate, it typically requires high computational power that the average Indonesian, often relying on budget devices, simply does not possess.

The Solution

We built SignLingo to make learning accessible to everyone, everywhere. Since it is web-based, anyone can access it easily. To solve the hardware constraint, I researched and implemented a specialized lightweight Hybrid CNN-LSTM architecture. This ensures high accuracy without the need for expensive GPUs, allowing the AI to act as a personal tutor that gives instant feedback directly in the browser.

Real-time hand landmark detection using MobileNetV2 and LSTM.

Key Technical Implementation

  • Hybrid Model Architecture: We utilized MobileNetV2 for efficient spatial feature extraction combined with LSTM to capture the temporal dynamics of hand gestures.
  • Optimized for Low-Resource: By applying L2 regularization and post-training quantization, we compressed the model to under 25MB, enabling offline operation on devices with as little as 4GB RAM.

Gamified Learning

To move away from boring, passive learning, we designed SignLingo to be entertaining. We added "Duolingo-style" gamification elements—such as points, daily streaks, and vocabulary unlocks—to keep users engaged while the AI provides the real-time feedback of a private tutor.

User dashboard showing power-ups, health-points and daily streak.

The Outcome

The optimized model achieved 95.30% validation accuracy on the BISINDO alphabet. With an inference time of just 40–50 milliseconds, the project successfully proved that inclusive communication technology can be made accessible to low-resource communities.
